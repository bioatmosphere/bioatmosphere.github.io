Working from Home during the Coronavirus Pandemic

Bin Wang
Last updated on Mar 19, 2020 5 min read life
virus

This will be a post that i will be writing and updating during the coming weeks while working from home all because of the coronavirus pandemic, as a personal record of this global chaos.

In the evening preceding working from home becasue of UCI asking all to minimize campus presence, after setting up the work station an idea sudently poped up in my head: why not writing a blog about this speccial period? The reasons to do this are obvious, which renders the motivation strong:

  1. First of all, the fundamental reason leading to this decision is totally becasue of the coronavirus pandemic.
  2. Second,I am confident to say i never worked from home (never is not the correct word choice, especially as a scientist). By never, i mean i have never worked from home fromally since the 2nd year of my PhD back in 2015, only briefly, e.g., reading a paper on my phone or fixing my Github-hosted website very recently.

With these two reasons, I am thinking it would be interesing to record this period of working from home and offer my personal experience about this special phase of a pandemic in human history. And this idea accidentally echos what a UVa professor is trying to let his/her students do during this pandemic– Write it Down).

Day 1: March 16th, 2020 (A day of loss)

Not a productive day! In the morning tried to work on microbial drought legacy project with respect to data analysis, but did not go well. In the afternoon tried best to figure out a specific analysis needed to be done for a long term, and had a meeting with postdoc advisor. Working from home the 1st day was not as productive as usual in office.

Quite interesting to see during my two breaks from working how things are changing because of the virus. At noon, found a person designated holding the door for customers in a restaurant. In the evening, headed out to a grocery store, and everything was gone, especially the frozen food, which i eat a lot usually.

Day 2: March 17th, 2020 (The day started from noon)

I got up pretty late around 10:30. Before a take-out lunch, i only worked only about 1 hour. Being afraid of virus transmission, i finished my lunch in a nearby park, where people were scarce. Tried hard to really work on my project, in the afternoon i found the good feeling of working usually in office.

However, the situation of coronavirus is getting worse and worse. State and local level strategies are increasingly aggresive. I really started to worry about or feel a little afraid of the risk of getting infected.

The sitation is evoving very fast. Stay Alert.

Day 3: March 18th, 2020 (Air full of virus)

Things are changing very fast since yesterday. Locally, people and cars were much less and were even rarely been seen than yesterday in restaruants and parking lots. Globally, the cases continue to climb up.

In the mid-afternoon I went to a park after grabbinga coffe to get some fresh air. Though the spring is comming, but i feel the atmosphere is filled with virus, and we are immersed in the virus. What we inhale is just virus. What a feeling!

Day 4(March 19th, 2020): Here comes the spring

With intermittent rain all day, spring finally comes to Southern California.

A day without any speciality personally. However,the cases confirmed and death toll are soaring. The state of california is projected to have more than a half of its 30 million people would contract the virus( link), which is really alarming. In the afternoon, I drunk wine with my roommate and had a good conversion.

Here is a photo of the pacific ocean near Newport Coast while i went to grocery store for some frozen food.

Day 5(March 20th, 2020): Warning against over-optimism

With five days into working from home, I have adjusted to working by my bed. And it’s the first day that i did not head out besides tossing garbage.

Locally, cases are increasing in Orange County. At the state level, California has issued a directive of asking people to shelter in palce. Here is a drone view of the streets in downtown Los Angeles. What an unbelievable scene!

Day 6(March 21st, 2020): First weekend since isolation

Started the day receiving the news that UCI had the 1st confirmed case. Stayed at home for the whole day, working on projects and reading papers and witnessing the number of confirmed cases in US soaring. Nothing quite interesting to mention, but a post accidentally came across on Gist written by Chris Wanstrath, a co-creator of GitHub, is quite inspiring, though themed in computer science, programming, etc.for doing research regardless field in terms of side project and open science. Here I list a few excerpts:

Why having side projects?

“You should always have a side project, too. Side projects give you an outlet, provide a useful distraction, let you explore new ideas, learn new concepts, and generally give you the freedom to be unaccountable. You don’t have to worry about your boss, or your coworkers, or the damn commentators on Reddit. Just have some fun. Treat yourself.”

Why open source?

“Open source can make wonderful things happen for you, and not just financially but socially too."

Start a open-source side project?

“So, start a side project. Who knows where it will take you."
